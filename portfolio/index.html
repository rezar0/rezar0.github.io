Geofencing

Spearheaded a collaboration between editorial and product/engineering to build a geofencing solution to capture at-risk revenue from global traffic on the US domain.

STAR Breakdown:

  • Situation - Finder Global was downsizing 20+ domains and all the traffic would be hitting the US domain instead, but merchandising wasnâ€™t set up to handle international traffic, only US traffic.
  • Task - Come up with a solution to still monetize global traffic on the US domain and best serve readers without jeopardizing US compliance or changing the US user experience.
  • Action - I conceptualized and built a frontend geofencing solution as a proof of concept that rolled out on 90% of US pages, then paired with engineering/product to hand it off to their teams to properly roll into the tech stack at their convenience.
  • Result - More than $30k/month on average was captured on the US domain through global traffic that otherwise would have been lost.

JParty

JParty is a simple web app in the style of Jeopardy that I made because I had a friend qualify to compete on Jeopardy and I wanted to help them with a study resource. This web app ties into hundreds of thousands of real Jeopardy questions and was designed to be as fast and simple as possible.

Fun facts:

  • I used a combination of JS, Python (emphasis on Pandas) and CSS to build this web app.
  • JParty sources more than 630k real Jeopardy questions and maintains all important aspects of them like difficulty, category and (obviously) the answer.
  • Future plans: Improve the loading speed by chunking the data, set up a recurring monthly scrape to gather new questions and use localStorage to store questions the user got correct (set through self nomination).
